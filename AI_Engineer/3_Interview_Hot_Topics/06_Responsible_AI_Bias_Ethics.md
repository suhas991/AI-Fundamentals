# Responsible AI: Bias, Ethics, and Governance

---

## Why Responsible AI Matters

AI systems can scale both benefits and harms. Responsible AI ensures systems are fair, safe, transparent, and accountable.

---

## Types of Bias (Interview Must-Know)

1. Data bias
- Training data underrepresents or stereotypes certain groups.

2. Label bias
- Human annotation decisions embed subjective bias.

3. Measurement bias
- Proxy variables (for example zip code) encode protected attributes.

4. Deployment bias
- Model used in a context different from training assumptions.

---

## Core Responsible AI Principles

- Fairness: avoid unjustified disparate impact.
- Reliability and safety: predictable behavior under normal and edge conditions.
- Privacy and security: protect personal and sensitive data.
- Transparency: make system limitations and confidence clear.
- Accountability: assign ownership for decisions and incident handling.

---

## Mitigation Across the Lifecycle

### 1. Data stage
- Audit representation by demographic slices.
- Remove harmful artifacts and leakage.

### 2. Model stage
- Evaluate with fairness and robustness metrics.
- Stress test harmful prompt patterns.

### 3. Product stage
- Add policy filters and human escalation paths.
- Provide explanations and citation behavior when possible.

### 4. Operations stage
- Continuous monitoring for drift and emergent harms.
- Incident response process for harmful outputs.

---

## Governance Artifacts (Good Interview Signal)

- Model cards
- Data sheets for datasets
- Risk register
- Red-team reports
- Human oversight SOPs

---

## Practical Team Checklist

- Define unacceptable failure cases before launch.
- Track fairness and safety metrics in dashboards.
- Include diverse stakeholders in review.
- Keep human-in-the-loop for high-impact decisions.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Bias is a system-level issue, not only a model-level issue.
- Responsible AI requires policy, process, and technical controls.
- Continuous monitoring is essential because risk changes after deployment.
- The goal is not zero risk; it is managed, measurable, and accountable risk.
