# Artificial Intelligence (AI): Types and Categories

## What Is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the broad field of building systems that can perform tasks typically associated with human intelligence, such as reasoning, learning, planning, perception, and language understanding.

## AI by Scope of Intelligence

### 1. Narrow AI (Weak AI)

- Built for specific tasks in a limited domain
- Cannot generalize broadly outside its training or design
- Examples: spam filters, recommendation engines, speech assistants
- This is the form of AI used in nearly all real-world products today

### 2. General AI (AGI / Strong AI)

- Hypothetical human-level intelligence across many domains
- Would adapt to new tasks with limited additional training
- Not yet achieved

### 3. Superintelligent AI

- Hypothetical intelligence beyond top human performance in most domains
- Commonly discussed in long-term AI safety and alignment debates

## AI by Functional Capability

### Reactive Machines

- No memory of past states
- Respond only to current input
- Example: IBM Deep Blue (chess)

### Limited Memory

- Uses recent past information to make decisions
- Most modern AI systems belong here
- Example: many perception and planning modules in autonomous driving

### Theory of Mind (Research Stage)

- Intended to model beliefs, goals, and emotions of others
- Not yet realized as a mature general capability

### Self-Aware AI (Theoretical)

- Would include consciousness and self-modeling
- No current systems meet this definition

## AI by Technical Approach

### Symbolic AI

- Rule-based reasoning and explicit knowledge representations
- Strong in logic-heavy and well-structured domains
- Examples: expert systems, planning engines, theorem provers

### Statistical AI

- Learns patterns from data under uncertainty
- Includes machine learning and deep learning methods
- Dominant approach in modern AI applications

## Core AI Domains

- **Computer Vision**: Understanding images and video
- **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**: Understanding and generating language
- **Robotics**: Perception, control, and autonomous physical action
- **Expert and Decision Systems**: Domain-specific reasoning and support

## Key Takeaways

1. AI is an umbrella field with multiple paradigms.
2. Today, almost all deployed systems are Narrow AI.
3. Most current progress comes from statistical methods, especially ML/DL.
4. AGI and superintelligence remain theoretical and policy-relevant topics.
